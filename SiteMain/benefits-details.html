Improve Customer Experience

Comfort Zone helps reduce fatigue and discomfort which contributes to a better employee experience and ultimately customer experience.

Employee and customer experience are very much entwined. Employees having pain and discomfort are less happy which can lead to poor customer experience. Comfort Zone provides companies with quantifiable return on investment in the form of productivity gains, avoided costs and improved employee experience which can lead to better customer experience.

 

Reduce Presenteeism

Lack of presenteeism adds up to 57.5 days per employee per year, 10x greater than absenteeism

According to Virgin Pulse data scientist, Dr. Olivia Sackett, employees take an average of four sick days off each year but days lost to presenteeism equaled 57.5 days, that’s almost 12 full working weeks that they are not performing at their best. From a dollar standpoint, presenteeism equates to $1.5 trillion in lost productivity. Comfort Zone reduces presenteeism by proactively recommending ergonomic changes that reduce worker discomfort and presenteeism.

 

Lower Costs

A company seeking a 3% profit margin will need an increase in sales of $2.1M to cover the cost of one carpel tunnel claim.

According to OSHA, occupational injuries and illnesses have an impact on a company’s profitability. OSHA developed a program that uses a company’s profit margin, the average cost of an injury or illness and an indirect cost multiplier to project the amount of sales a company would need to generate to cover these costs. Comfort Zone can help identify and eliminate occupational injuries that can result in lower profitability.  https://www.osha.gov/dcsp/smallbusiness/safetypays/estimator.html

 

Improve Productivity

Disengaged employees cost $450-550B in lost productivity annually.

According to a Gallup poll, disengaged employees caused US companies between $450-550B in lost productivity every year. Comfort Zone helps reduce fatigue and discomfort keeping employees happier and more engaged through better working environment.

Muscularsketal disorders (MSDs) account for 20% of total healthcare costs, 34% of lost workdays 1 out of 3 dollars spent on workers comp and billions of dollars in lost productivity. Comfort Zone identifies risk and discomfort so companies can proactively lower these and other costs.

Absenteeism averages 5 sick days per year. Lack of presenteeism adds up to 57.5 days per employee per year, 10x greater loss in productivity than absenteeism. Comfort Zone helps identify the contributing factors that lead to pain and discomfort so they can be addressed to improve presenteeism.
